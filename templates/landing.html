Problem Statement

Theory of Computation remains one of the most abstract and challenging subjects in computer science education. Students struggle to visualize finite automata, understand state transitions, and grasp the equivalence between different computational models. Traditional teaching methods rely on static diagrams and manual trace execution, making it difficult to develop intuition for automata behavior and language recognition.

Theoretical Foundations

Hopcroft's Minimization

O(n log n) algorithm for DFA state reduction through partition refinement

Subset Construction

Rabin-Scott algorithm for determinizing non-deterministic automata

Thompson's Construction

Compositional NFA building from regular expression parse trees

Epsilon Closure

Transitive closure computation for ε-transition reachability
